Crown Shymess

From Wikipedia, the free encylopedia

For the album by trash boat, see Crown Shyness

Crown Shymess(also canopy disengagement,[1] canopy shyness,[2] or inter-crown spacing [3]) is a phenomenon observed in some tree species, in which the crowns of fully stocked trees do not touch each other, forming a canopy with channel-like gaps. [4][5] The phenomenon is most prevalent among trees of the same species, but also occurs between trees of different species.[6][7] There exist many hypotheses as to why crown shyness is an adaptive behavior, and research suggests that it might inhibit spread of leaf-eating insect larvae.[8]
